Intro

During school, a student getting taught new subjects can be confusing.

Now the girl is confused and questioning where she fits in her roles, but it goes against what she believed as an individual.

When she goes home, she tells her parents of what she learning. They become upset, especially over the lack of control over her education despite their parental rights.

Resulting in the new Executive Order, with the intention of ending radical indoctrination,

That is Executive Order #14190

Information

This Executive Order was made to take control over radical subjects being taught in school.

With many specific goals in mind, that came in the process of eliminating the indoctrination.

The plans were two different paths of action. One, to have General/State attorney generals formulate a plan to monitor curriculum and two, to continue the federal fund freeze.

People who agreed with the Executive Order believed that it would stop the insistent division, confusion, and distrust that came with the new topic.

People who disagreed with the Executive Order believed that having those new subjects implemented into the learning environment meant creating an open and educated community.

However, surveys and data found that teachers and adults alike desired subjects of race to be involved in education, but subjects of gender and sexual orientation to be outside of school.

Thank you for listening.
